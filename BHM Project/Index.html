Evelyn Boyd Granville

Born on May 1, 1924, in Washington, D.C., Evelyn Boyd Granville was a trailblazer in the field of mathematics. She earned her Ph.D. in mathematics from Yale University in 1949, becoming the second African American Woman to earn a Ph.D. in mathematics in the United States.
Her work as in Education, Mathematics, and Computer Science most notably with her significant contributions to NASA's space program during the Apollo missions, where she worked on trajectory analysis and orbital mechanics. Creating groundbeaking computer software to analyze satellite orbits and trajectories, which was crucial for the success of the Apollo missions. Her work helped ensure the safe return of astronauts to Earth and contributed to the overall success of the space program.
